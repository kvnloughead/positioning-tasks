If you looked through the element inspector you would have found that the second child didn't disappear at all â€” it moved to the top of its parent container, and was obscured by the first child. This is what happens when you apply position: absolute to an element: it stays where it is, but it's neighbors act as if it wasn't there.

Like with relative position, when an element is absolutely positioned you can use top, right, bottom and left properties to adjust its position. But there's a difference. Instead of shifting their position with respect to itself, you shift it with respect to its closest positioned ancestor.
